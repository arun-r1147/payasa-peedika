PALADA PAYASAM

Palada Payasam is a traditional South Indian dessert, particularly popular in the state of Kerala. Palada payasam is creamy and thick with a slightly chewy texture from the ada. The rich flavor of milk, the sweetness of sugar, and the warmth of cardamom make it a delightful dessert.

Palada payasam is loved for its simplicity and rich, comforting flavors, making it a staple in Kerala's culinary traditions.

PARIPPU PAYASAM

Parippu Payasam is a classic dessert from Kerala, South India. It is a sweet, creamy pudding made from moong dal (yellow lentils), jaggery, and coconut milk, flavored with cardamom and garnished with roasted coconut, cashews, and raisins.

This payasam is not only delicious but also embodies the essence of Kerala's culinary traditions, showcasing the rich flavors of jaggery and coconut, which are staples in Kerala cuisine.

GOTHAMBU PAYASAM

Gothambu Payasam (also known as Wheat Payasam) is a traditional dessert from Kerala, made using broken wheat jaggery, and coconut milk.oconut, creates a harmonious and comforting dessert. The cardamom adds a fragrant note, while the ghee-roasted cashews and raisins offer a delightful crunch.

Gothambu Payasam is cherished for its rustic flavor and wholesome ingredients, making it a beloved dish in Kerala's culinary repertoire.
